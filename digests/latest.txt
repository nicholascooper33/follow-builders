Unsupervised Learning Ep 85: Has AI Infra Stabilized, FM Vibe Shift & What's Next for Coding Agents
https://www.youtube.com/@RedpointAI

Takeaway: The AI coding wars are massive and still accelerating — and 2026 is shaping up to be the year coding agents break containment beyond code into everything else.

In this annual crossover episode between Unsupervised Learning (Redpoint's Jacob Efron) and Latent Space (Swyx), the two dig into AI infrastructure stability, the coding wars, foundation model dynamics, open vs. closed models, and what comes next. The episode is unusually data-rich: Swyx estimates Anthropic is at roughly $2.5B ARR just from Claude Code, OpenAI's coding revenue is similarly estimated, and Cursor is rumored at $2B ARR — all created in roughly one year. "Both OpenAI and Anthropic have made it their priority to compete on coding," Swyx notes.

On infrastructure stability: Swyx says building patterns have landed on a consensus — agents are LLMs with tools in a loop, a filesystem, and a skill layer (minimal markdown files with scripts attached). But he cautions against staking a reputation on calling stability "permanent" — the real adaptation still ahead is around memory, real-time elements, and sub-agents. Jacob draws the infrastructure/application divide sharply: app companies can afford to throw things out every three months because they have sticky end customers; infra companies face complete churn when they fall behind.

On coding competition: Claude Code remains the first-mover leader on vibes and stickiness; Codex is catching up on speed and integrations. The open question is where Gemini and XAI fit in — both are trying, neither has broken through yet. Microsoft's GitHub position is flagged as a sleeping giant. Chinese labs (ZAI, GLM) are also pushing hard for a piece of the market.

On the next frontier, Swyx introduces the concept of "dark factories" — not just zero human-written code, but zero human code review. A handful of companies are beginning to explore this, and he argues it is the only truly scalable path: "You just have to flip the SDLC entirely." He also notes that 60% of traffic to Vercel's app admin architecture is now bots, not humans — your primary customer is increasingly an agent.

On open models, Swyx reversed his earlier skepticism: the top 20% of builders are moving toward open models, driven by cost, fine-tuning, and the rise of alternative inference chips (Cerebras, Thales) enabling thousands of tokens per second.

Direct quote: "The general thesis that I have been pursuing now is that the same way that 2025 was a year of coding agents, 2026 is coding agents breaking containment, to do everything else."
https://www.youtube.com/@RedpointAI

Anthropic Engineering: An update on recent Claude Code quality reports
https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/april-23-postmortem

Anthropic published a full postmortem on three separate issues that degraded Claude Code quality over the past month, all resolved as of April 20 (v2.1.116).

Issue 1 — Reasoning effort downgrade (March 4): Default reasoning was quietly lowered from "high" to "medium" to reduce long-tail latency. Users reported Claude feeling less intelligent. Reversed April 7; all users now default to xhigh effort on Opus 4.7 and high on all other models.

Issue 2 — Caching bug dropped prior thinking history (March 26): A caching optimization had a bug that continuously wiped Claude's prior reasoning from sessions instead of doing it just once after an idle period. This made Claude appear forgetful, repetitive, and caused unusual tool-choice behavior. It also drained usage limits faster than expected. Fixed April 10. Notably, Opus 4.7 caught the bug during back-testing while Opus 4.6 did not.

Issue 3 — System prompt verbosity cap (April 16): A prompt addition capping responses at 25 words between tool calls hurt coding quality. Reverted April 20.

Going forward, Anthropic is resetting usage limits for all subscribers as of April 23, adding tighter controls on system prompt changes, expanding eval coverage, and improving the Code Review tool they use internally. They've also created the ClaudeDevs account on X for more transparent product communication.
https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/april-23-postmortem
