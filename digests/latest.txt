Training Data — "From SEO to Agent-Led Growth: Profound's James Cadwallader"
Published: April 14, 2026
https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLOhHNjZItNnMm5tdW61JpnyxeYH5NDDx8

Takeaway: The front door of the internet hasn't changed — but who's walking through it has, and that shift from human consumer to AI agent is rewriting the entire rulebook for marketing.

James Cadwallader, co-founder and CEO of Profound (a marketing platform serving close to 12% of the Fortune 500), joined the Training Data podcast to explain why agent-led growth (ALG) is fundamentally different from SEO — and why most marketers are still pattern-matching the new world to the old one.

The core argument: when a person Googled something, they scanned the top five blue links because human attention is scarce. When an AI agent answers a query, it might crawl 65 web pages to formulate a response. That's not a marginal change — it's an orders-of-magnitude expansion of the internet's surface area that matters for discovery. The implication for marketers is that content designed purely to rank for human eyes now needs to be legible and useful to superintelligent agents with "infinite bandwidth."

James also broke down why different AI platforms behave differently as recommendation engines: Gemini leans heavily on YouTube (Google owns it), ChatGPT tends to pull from Reddit for consumer queries and LinkedIn for B2B, and Claude has recently become more sensitive to real-time web information. Profound helps brands understand not just whether they appear in AI responses, but what sentiment and citations surround those appearances — and then deploys agents to close the gaps.

The most provocative idea in the episode: content "slop" is a red herring. The real challenge is telling a superintelligent model something it doesn't already know. As James put it, "humans are this kind of fleshy API between reality and the Internet at this point" — which means original insight, proprietary data, and first-hand experience are what differentiate marketing content in the agent era.

Direct quote:
"The fundamental difference between SEO and this new world is that in SEO, you were building content that was designed to be picked up by an algorithm, but fundamentally consumed by a human. Whereas in this new world, you are building content that is frankly designed entirely to be both discovered and then consumed by an agent." — James Cadwallader

Claude Blog — "Redesigning Claude Code on desktop for parallel agents"
Published: April 14, 2026
https://claude.com/blog/claude-code-desktop-redesign

The new Claude Code desktop app is built around the reality that agentic coding now means many things running at once. The redesign introduces a sidebar for managing multiple sessions across repos, a drag-and-drop layout for arranging your workspace, and an integrated terminal and file editor — so you can review, tweak, and ship without bouncing to a separate tool. The diff viewer has been rebuilt for performance on large changesets, and HTML/PDF preview is now in-app. Three view modes (Verbose, Normal, Summary) let you dial between full transparency into Claude's tool calls and just the results. The app now also has full parity with CLI plugins, and SSH support extends to Mac. Available now for Pro, Max, Team, and Enterprise plans.
