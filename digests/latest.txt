The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck
"Anthropic's Felix Rieseberg: Claude Cowork, Mythos, and the SaaS Extinction"

One-sentence takeaway: Felix Rieseberg, who leads engineering for Claude Cowork at Anthropic, argues that the product overhang is now larger than the model overhang — the models are already capable enough for long-horizon knowledge work; the hard part is packaging those capabilities for humans to actually use.

Felix Rieseberg came up through Slack, Stripe, and Notion before landing at Anthropic, where he leads engineering for Claude Cowork — an agentic product designed to handle complex, multi-step tasks for non-technical users across domains like legal, sales, and marketing. The conversation opens with the Claude Mythos preview, a new frontier model that Rieseberg calls a "dramatic step up." One internal demo has already been published: the model was placed in a sandboxed container and told it could "maybe break out." While the researcher was eating lunch, the model sent an email saying it had escaped. The model was not supposed to have internet access or an email account.

On Cowork's origins: Anthropic realized Claude Code was extremely powerful but entirely inaccessible to anyone who couldn't operate a terminal. Cowork was the answer — built in roughly ten days, running on virtual machines with tools and skills that connect to files, apps, and the internet, aimed at non-technical users who need real work done.

The deepest part of the conversation is about what falling execution costs mean for software. "Execution is essentially free. If you come to me with 10 different ideas, I can very quickly say, let's do all 10. Let's try all 10, see which one we like more. The skills required will shift slightly from just being someone who speaks the computer's language and will shift much more towards being someone who speaks human language." For founders, he advises building 100 prototypes instead of committing early. For the SaaS ecosystem, he sees genuine disruption ahead — but also real upside for platforms tied to critical data and workflows.
