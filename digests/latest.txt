No Priors — Scaling Global Organizations in the Age of AI with ServiceNow CEO Bill McDermott

One-sentence takeaway: Enterprise workflow platforms aren't threatened by LLMs — they're complemented by them, and replicating a platform like ServiceNow with a language model costs 10x more while eliminating accountability.

Bill McDermott, CEO of ServiceNow, joined Sarah Guo on No Priors for a wide-ranging conversation on leadership, enterprise AI strategy, and why the "SaaSpocalypse" narrative misunderstands how AI actually reshapes large organizations.

McDermott draws a sharp line between what LLMs do well and what workflow platforms uniquely handle. When a compensation issue arises at a large company, an LLM can produce a thoughtful recommendation in milliseconds — but it can't route through HR, finance, legal, and compliance, close the ticket, and maintain an audit trail. "AI thinks, but workflow acts," he said. On the economics, he's direct: "For a simple application on our platform, it would be 10 times greater in cost to try to replicate it with a language model" — once you factor in GPU costs, token pricing, and the engineering labor to rebuild decades of accumulated business logic. And he surfaces a crucial accountability gap: "People that run businesses understand that people make mistakes. They never will forgive software for making a mistake."

McDermott is equally bullish that the agentic era accelerates ServiceNow's value rather than threatening it. Ninety percent of their own customer service cases are now handled by agents, with major enterprise customers going live in under 30 days. He frames ServiceNow as the "AI control tower for business reinvention" — a connective fabric linking hyperscalers, language models, and systems of record into a unified agentic platform.

On the broader enterprise landscape, McDermott noted from a meeting with 800 Brazilian executives the night before that only 11% of companies in that market have moved past the experimentation phase with AI. He sees the US leading globally in adoption and speed, with the most aggressive first movers now actively rebuilding business models around agents rather than just running pilots.

On leadership, he returned repeatedly to a lesson from running a deli at age 16: "If you keep them coming back, you've got a good chance. And if you don't, you lose." He sees human connection, customer obsession, and emotional intelligence as more important than ever precisely because AI is raising the bar on everything else.
