Latent Space — "Mistral: Voxtral TTS, Forge, Leanstral, & what's next for Mistral 4 — w/ Pavan Kumar Reddy & Guillaume Lample"
Published: March 30, 2026

Takeaway: Mistral is building a full audio stack from scratch using a novel autoregressive flow-matching architecture — delivering a 3B-parameter TTS model that rivals larger competitors at a fraction of the cost.

Latent Space sat down with Guillaume Lample (Chief Scientist at Mistral) and Pavan Kumar Reddy (Head of Audio Research) to discuss Voxtral TTS, Mistral's first text-to-speech model. It supports nine languages, is built on top of the Minestral base model, and runs as a lightweight 3B-parameter model designed for speed and cost efficiency. Weights are being released publicly, though not fully open.

The key technical distinction is an autoregressive flow-matching architecture paired with a novel in-house neural audio codec that converts speech into a mix of semantic and acoustic latent tokens. Pavan explained why flow matching outperforms simpler approaches: "Even the same word could be inflected in so many different ways. Any approach which models this distribution — flow matching is one which works pretty reasonably well. You have to pick one and then infer sharply. You can't predict the mean of it because that corresponds to some blurred out speech."

Guillaume contextualized the broader product strategy: Mistral is building Forge, a fine-tuning platform that now supports audio models, enabling enterprises to adapt Voxtral for domain-specific vocabulary, noisy acoustic environments, new languages, and voice personalization. Each enterprise wants a different tone and personality, and the fine-tuning infrastructure is the moat. The roadmap is deliberate — transcription first, then generation, with full-duplex (simultaneous speech in and out) as a future milestone. Guillaume noted that unlike pure language model research, audio still has wide-open design space: "There are so many ways to actually build this model... the flow matching turned out to be quite more natural."
