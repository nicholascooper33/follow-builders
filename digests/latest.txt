AI & I by Every — "The AI Model Built for What LLMs Can't Do"
Published: 2026-04-15
https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLuMcoKK9mKgHtW_o9h5sGO2vXrffKHwJL

Takeaway: Energy-based models (EBMs) offer a fundamentally different and potentially safer path for AI in mission-critical applications — one that doesn't rely on token prediction and can be formally constrained to follow hard rules.

Dan Shipper (CEO of Every) interviewed Eve, founder and CEO of Logical Intelligence, about why the company is building what they call Kona — formally, an "energy based reasoning model with latent variables" — as an alternative to the LLM paradigm.

The central argument is architectural. LLMs predict the next token in a sequence, one step at a time, with no ability to backtrack or see the full picture. This makes them prone to hallucination and expensive to run. Eve describes it as having "tunnel vision" while navigating a city — you can only turn one direction at a time and might walk into a hole you can see but can't avoid. EBMs, by contrast, work by mapping all possible states into an energy landscape and navigating toward the lowest-energy (most probable) outcome — giving them a "bird's eye view" that lets them reroute when something goes wrong.

Because EBMs don't use tokens, they're cheaper to run and better suited for tasks that have nothing to do with language: spatial reasoning, applied engineering, data analysis, chip design, circuit control. Eve points out that LLMs are fundamentally "literature department" tools being applied to "engineering school" problems — it works, but it's wasteful. EBMs also have the unusual property of being inspectable during training, not just after, making them less of a black box. And crucially, they can be formally constrained: you can force an EBM to follow a hard set of rules, something you simply can't do with an LLM.

Logical Intelligence's strategy is not to replace LLMs but to complement them. As Eve puts it: "You want to make them cheaper. Everyone wants. You can outsource the task to us related to spatial reasoning." The duo also discussed why investment has been slow to shift — billions are already committed to the LLM ecosystem and a huge amount of circular dependency has built up between model companies, data centers, and hardware. But Eve noted that some major LLM companies are quietly building EBMs in-house — which she reads as a positive signal for the paradigm.

Direct quote from Eve: "EBMs are naturally non autoregressive. There are no sequences of tokens, and that's what makes it fundamentally different. Like, imagine you're trying to navigate the map, and you have a left brain. To navigate, you're sort of allowed to choose one direction of the time. And sometimes you take the wrong turns just because you hallucinate... EBM gonna have the bird view all the time. So if you see there's a hole, you're gonna choose a different route."
