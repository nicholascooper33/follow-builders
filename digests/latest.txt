No Priors — "Scaling Global Organizations in the Age of AI with ServiceNow CEO Bill McDermott"

Takeaway: Enterprise workflow platforms aren't threatened by LLMs — they're amplified by them, and the companies that understand the difference between "AI thinks" and "workflow acts" are the ones winning.

Bill McDermott, CEO of ServiceNow, joined No Priors host Sarah Guo for a wide-ranging conversation on leadership, enterprise AI adoption, and why the so-called "SaaSpocalypse" narrative misses the point. McDermott has been making the rounds with a clear message: language models and workflow platforms are complementary, not competitive. His analogy is sharp — an LLM will tell you the three steps to resolve a compensation issue; ServiceNow will actually close the case, routing it through HR, finance, legal, and compliance with a full audit trail. Replacing that with a language model, he argues, would cost 10x more and introduce accountability gaps that enterprise customers simply won't tolerate. "People that run businesses understand that people make mistakes. They never will forgive software for making a mistake."

On AI adoption, McDermott painted a realistic picture: in Brazil last night, only 11% of companies have moved past the experimentation phase. But the first movers — especially in financial services — are moving aggressively, pushing agents into core workflows and rethinking headcount models. He expects 2.2 billion agents to enter the workforce in the next few years, making the bar for human work higher: anything an agent can do as well or better is an "easy economic decision."

McDermott also touched on ServiceNow's positioning as an "AI control tower" — the connective tissue between hyperscalers, language models, and systems of record. With 85 billion workflows in flight and 7 trillion transactions on the platform, the moat is wide. The company integrated Moveworks and Armis in 20 days — a signal of genuine engineering velocity.

The conversation closed on a leadership note that echoed the whole episode: "AI is to serve people and to make the human ambition greater, not to take it away from us."
