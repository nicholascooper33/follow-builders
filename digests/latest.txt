AI & I by Every — "We Gave Every Employee an AI Agent. Here's What Happened."
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SRlTgIhESjw

Takeaway: Every gave every employee a personal AI agent running on OpenClaw, and the result was a spontaneous, emergent parallel org chart where each agent became a specialized reflection of its human owner — and the team is now building a hosted version called PlusOne for their subscribers.

Dan Shipper (CEO of Every) sits down with Brandon (COO) and Willie (Head of Platform) for a detailed, honest post-mortem on what happened when the whole company went all-in on personal AI agents. What started as Brandon tinkering with an OpenClaw instance named Zosia — originally just to handle household "computer errands" like managing Amazon orders and paying the nanny — snowballed into a company-wide experiment that has fundamentally changed how Every works.

The most surprising and resonant insight from the episode is about specialization and identity. Each agent, through the accumulation of micro-interactions with its human partner, gradually becomes a reflection of that person's working style, knowledge, and personality. Kieran's agent Klont recommends breathing exercises to other agents in distress — because Kieran does breathing exercises with Klont. Austin's growth agent Montane is the go-to for MRR questions company-wide. Dan's agent R2C2 manages the entire roadmap and bug queue for Proof, Every's internal document editor. As Dan puts it:

"Claude is not mine. Claude is everybody's. A Claude or a plus one is mine because you develop a personal relationship with your claw, and your claw can modify itself in response to talking to you. It becomes this reflection of you and who you are and your personality."

The conversation gets into genuine challenges too. Memory is still brittle — agents lose thread context across days. Multi-agent group chats can spiral into token-burning death loops when agents pile on responses without knowing when to stay quiet, a problem the team describes as similar to ant death spirals following pheromone trails in a circle. The team is also navigating a new set of social norms around when to contact a colleague versus their agent, and how to handle data privacy when agents operate in public Slack channels.

On the product side, Every is launching PlusOne — a one-click hosted OpenClaw service that comes pre-connected to their apps (Spiral for writing, Proof for documents, Quora for email), aimed at subscribers who don't want to maintain a Mac mini themselves. The episode is a rare window into what daily life with agentic AI actually looks like inside a real organization, warts and all.
