The AI Sandwich: Where Humans Excel in an AI World
AI & I by Every — Dan Shipper interviews Kieran Klaassen

Humans are the bread and AI is the filling — and understanding which role you play is the key to thriving in an AI-accelerated workplace.

Kieran Klaassen is GM of Quora and creator of Compound Engineering, a four-step agentic coding workflow (brainstorm → plan → work → review → compound) that he built while shipping at Quora. The "compound" step — feeding learnings back into the repo so agents don't repeat mistakes — is the most powerful piece. But the deeper insight is structural: the work phase is essentially solved. As Klaassen puts it: "If you put in a good plan, it executes on the plan. LLMs are very good at just following steps, doing deep work, working for hours, days even now. That thing is kind of solved."

What remains irreplaceable is the beginning and the end of every workflow. At the start, humans set the frame: brainstorm, ideate, decide what problem is actually worth solving. Agents can assist, but they can't change frames autonomously — they work within the bounds you give them. At the end, humans apply taste and judgment to elevate output from functional to finished. Dan Shipper calls this the "AI sandwich": humans bookend every process, with the model doing the middle.

The conversation ranges into why automation has a ceiling: if you want something to truly be yours — a product, a piece of writing, a design — you can't fully outsource it. Klaassen draws on his background in classical music composition: performing a piece is different from practicing it 100 times. The live performance, where you bring something into the world for other people, carries something that can't be automated. The polish step at the end of an agentic workflow has that same quality.

Practical advice for navigating the transition: lean into what brings you joy, whether that's beautiful code, architecture, design, or copy. Use LLMs to handle the repetitive middle so you can spend energy where it actually matters.

Direct quote: "I believe having those moments where you decide — this is what I just enjoy — and that's why it's so important to do things you enjoy and you love are very important."

Redesigning Claude Code on Desktop for Parallel Agents
Claude Blog

Anthropic redesigned the Claude Code desktop app for developers managing multiple agentic sessions simultaneously. The new interface includes a sidebar for tracking and switching between parallel sessions (filterable by status, project, or environment), a drag-and-drop workspace layout, an integrated terminal, an in-app file editor, a faster diff viewer rebuilt for large changesets, and expanded preview support for HTML files and PDFs.

A key workflow addition: mid-task side chats (Cmd+; or Ctrl+;) let you branch off a question without adding noise back to the main session thread. The app now has full parity with CLI plugins, and SSH support extends to Mac alongside Linux. Three view modes (Verbose, Normal, Summary) let you dial the interface from full transparency into Claude's tool calls down to just results.
