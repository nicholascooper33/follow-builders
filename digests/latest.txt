Guillermo Rauch — CEO, Vercel

Rauch issued a detailed public statement about a major security incident at Vercel. A Vercel employee's account was compromised through the breach of an AI platform they were using, which allowed a sophisticated attacker to escalate access into Vercel's internal environments. Unsupervised Learning — Ep 84: OpenAI's Chief Scientist on Continual Learning Hype, RL Beyond Code & Future Alignment Directions

OpenAI's Chief Scientist isn't worried about the "continual learning" hype cycle — because, in his view, continual learning is exactly what they've been building toward all along.

In a wide-ranging conversation with Jacob Efron on Unsupervised Learning, the Chief Scientist covered the current state of model capabilities, what it will take to reach autonomous AI researchers, and why alignment is more tractable now than it seemed just a few years ago.

On model progress: He called the explosive rise of coding tools — now used for "the majority of actual coding" at OpenAI — a clear signal that something is on track. He expects gradual increases in model autonomy over the next 3–6 months, with models capable of working autonomously for a couple of days arriving soon, producing "much higher quality artifacts on their own."

On RL beyond coding and math: He sees reinforcement learning expanding to harder-to-verify domains like medicine, law, and finance, framing it as the same challenge as longer-horizon tasks — where even well-specified problems require open-ended daily decisions. He's optimistic about scaling RL to general domains, but flagged that in-context learning may ultimately be more efficient for most business use cases than building custom RL pipelines.

On alignment: He highlighted chain-of-thought monitoring as a powerful and underrated alignment tool. Because chain-of-thought is not directly supervised during training, it can reveal a model's actual reasoning in ways that final outputs cannot. "Allowing the models some sort of private space... the important thing is not supervising [the chain of thought] during training. If we established a paradigm where you just shout these chains of thought in product, eventually you kind of have to train them." His broader view: alignment has moved from a "nebulous problem" toward concrete technical progress, but his timelines to very capable models have shortened — requiring the industry to be prepared to make hard trade-offs.

On AI for science: He shared that OpenAI's models have already solved problems from a "First Proof" challenge — problems designed by expert mathematicians to represent real research work, with no published solutions. "Seeing the model come up with these ideas, which I would be quite proud to come up with in a week or two, seeing it come up with them in an hour or so — that was a very weird feeling."

On governance: His biggest societal concern isn't job displacement per se, but highly capable automated organizations controlled by a very small number of people — and the lack of any clear governance framework for that scenario. "What does governance of such things look like? What are these organizations that are so powerful and yet maybe made of only a couple of people?"
