No Priors: "The Agentic Economy: How AI Agents Will Transform the Financial System" with Jeremy Allaire (Co-Founder & CEO, Circle)
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eyobeqMdbeI

Takeaway: The agentic economy is being born right now, and existing financial infrastructure — built for humans — cannot support the billions of micro-transactions, real-time settlements, and dynamically-spawned financial endpoints that AI agents will require.

Jeremy Allaire joined No Priors to argue that stablecoins and blockchain infrastructure aren't just crypto novelties — they're the necessary financial plumbing for a world where AI agents autonomously conduct economic activity. He traced Circle's origin to 2013, when he believed the internet needed a "protocol for dollars": full-reserve, programmable money that could move instantly and globally at near-zero cost. USDC, now codified in US law under the Genius Act, is that vision realized.

The bulk of the conversation turned to what Allaire calls the agentic economy. As AI agents begin doing more and more of the white-collar economy's work — consuming services from each other, paying for specialized intelligence, settling contracts — the financial system needs infrastructure that works like the internet: always on, globally interoperable, programmable by any piece of software, and capable of scaling to trillions of transactions. "We need transactions that can scale potentially into the billions or trillions... We need that to work in real time, again, between any piece of hardware, software, anywhere in the world." Legacy banking systems, he argues, cannot do this.

Circle's answer is ARC, which Allaire describes as an "economic operating system" for this machine-driven economy. Unlike earlier blockchains designed for censorship resistance outside the financial mainstream, ARC is built with a known validator set of major financial infrastructure companies, deterministic settlement finality in hundreds of milliseconds, and USDC as the native token — real dollars, no volatile gas token. Privacy primitives are built in from day one.

Allaire is also bullish on tokenized real-world assets (tokenized stocks, treasuries, euros) and on-chain prediction markets, noting that USDC already powers Polymarket. On the GDP question, he thinks double-digit growth in the 2030s is "not unrealistic" — but warns the real risk is that AI-driven GDP growth concentrates at the capital level rather than flowing to humans, making the construction of a new social contract the defining challenge of the coming decade.

Direct quote: "Agentic economic activity is actually how does the organization of what we used to think of as labor and capital — how does this organization of kind of compute work happen, and what kinds of corporate forms might emerge in that world to do that."

Claude Blog: "Preparing your security program for AI-accelerated offense"
https://claude.com/blog/preparing-your-security-program-for-ai-accelerated-offense

Anthropic's security engineering and research teams published a practical, prioritized guide for security programs in the age of AI-accelerated attacks. The core warning: within the next 24 months, frontier AI models will find and chain bugs that have sat unnoticed in production code for years — and attackers will use the same tools first unless defenders move fast.

The seven priority areas, ranked by urgency: (1) Close your patch gap — patch KEV catalog items immediately, use EPSS to prioritize the rest, target 24-hour patch windows for internet-facing systems. (2) Handle a much higher volume of vulnerability reports — plan for an order-of-magnitude increase in findings, automate triage with AI in the loop. (3) Find bugs before you ship — add AI-assisted code review and automated penetration testing to CI/CD pipelines, adopt memory-safe languages for new code. (4) Find vulnerabilities already in your code — proactively scan your own codebase with a frontier model before attackers do, prioritizing code that parses untrusted input or enforces auth decisions. (5) Design for breach — zero trust architecture, hardware-bound credentials, short-lived tokens; mitigations that only work through friction are now much less effective against AI-powered attackers. (6) Reduce and inventory your exposed surface — maintain a real-time inventory, decommission legacy services, use AI to identify unused endpoints and run autonomous external red-teaming. (7) Shorten incident response time — put a model at the front of your alert queue, automate incident bookkeeping, run tabletop exercises that simulate five simultaneous incidents in one week.

The post also includes specific guidance for submitting vulnerability reports responsibly — including the advice to disclose AI involvement upfront, provide a working reproduction, and include a proposed patch — as open-source maintainers are already overwhelmed by low-quality automated reports.
